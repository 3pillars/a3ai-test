# AI & Human Expertise - Key Insights

Source: Sebastian Bubeck (OpenAI) via summary

## Core Thesis
- Raw compute becoming commodity
- Deep human expertise = ultimate moat
- AI won't equalize human capability - it will DIVIDE those who understand vs those who just consume

## Key Quotes
- "Expertise and deep expertise in a scientific field is more important than ever"
- "If you don't have foundational understanding, you cannot push the model past its surface"
- "Trapped in a loop: typing prompts, getting answers, understanding neither, building nothing"

## The Fracture
- Not AI vs no-AI
- **Understanding deeply enough to direct** vs **just consume whatever it produces**
- One group builds, the other gets replaced

## The Trap
- Machine makes hard study feel unnecessary
- But comprehension must be precise enough to tell machine where it's wrong
- That knowledge doesn't come from prompting - comes from years of hard study

## Implication for Jacob
- Learn foundational: math, physics, engineering
- Don't just prompt - understand the problem deeply
- CS50 AI course is a good start
- Combine AI tool + deep domain knowledge = unstoppable
